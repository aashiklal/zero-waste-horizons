Why is Proper Disposal Important?

Every day, hazardous waste finds its way into our environment, posing severe risks. From contaminating our water sources to threatening wildlife and even human health, the repercussions of improper disposal are alarming.By understanding and practicing safe disposal, we not only protect nature but also ensure a healthier community for us and future generations.

Common Hazardous Waste in Victorian Households

Batteries

Batteries contain chemicals like cadmium, mercury, and lead. When improperly discarded, they can contaminate soil and water sources.

Paints

Leftover paints, especially oil-based ones, contain volatile organic compounds (VOCs) that can pollute air and water.

Fluorescent Lights

Fluorescent lights contain mercury, a heavy metal that can contaminate air and water, harming ecosystems and human health if not disposed of correctly.

White Goods

Older white goods may contain harmful substances such as lead, mercury and cadmium. White goods used for refrigeration may contain Freon (HCFCs, HCFCs.) When improperly discarded, they can contaminate soil and water sources and the atmosphere.

E-Waste

Electronic devices often contain hazardous materials, including heavy metals such as lead, mercury, cadmium, and chromium. These metals can be highly toxic to both humans and the environment.

Mercury thermometer

Mercury thermometer contain mercury, a heavy metal that can contaminate air and water, harming ecosystems and human health if not disposed of correctly.
