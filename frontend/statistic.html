Waste Management Visualization

The Australian Government's biennial "2022 National Waste Report", released at the end of 2022, found that waste generated by Australians increased by nearly 3% compared to 2018/19. In recent years, Australia as a whole and particularly the state of Victoria have faced an increasingly serious waste management challenge. According to statistics from 2022, in 2020/21, Victoria produced about 3.48 million tonnes of household waste.

Despite the state government implementing a range of measures, such as promoting waste sorting, intensifying punishments for illegal waste dumping, and raising environmental awareness among residents, Victoria has seen continuous improvement in its waste recycling and conversion rates. However, it still confronts numerous challenges, including incomplete waste sorting, inadequate waste management facilities, and the persistence of illegal waste dumping.

This page will help you quickly understand the waste collection situation of residents in various regions of Victoria, as well as the waste collection trends in Melbourne, the capital city of Victoria, in recent years. Lastly, we will also show you a detailed classification of different types of waste in Victoria and their conversion rates, helping you to better plan your waste management.

Melbourne, as the capital of Victoria, is one of Australia's cultural, artistic, and economic centers. However, with the rapid development of the city and the growth of the population, Melbourne also faces challenges in waste management. In recent years, the city's total waste has shown an upward trend, including household waste, plastic waste, etc. Currently, the government and local communities have taken a series of measures, such as promoting waste classification and recycling, strengthening the penalties for illegal waste dumping, and raising residents' environmental awareness. Now we'd like to introduce Melbourne, the capital of Victoria, as a typical example of urban waste collection.
